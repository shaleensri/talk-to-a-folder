Syllabus: Biology 10 - Living Systems

Course Description
Biology 10 introduces students to cells, genetics, evolution, ecology, and human body systems. The course emphasizes evidence-based reasoning, lab safety, data interpretation, and clear scientific writing.

Essential Questions
How do cells maintain life? How are traits inherited and expressed? How do populations change over time? How do organisms interact with their environments? How can biological evidence inform public health and conservation decisions?

Major Units
1. Scientific inquiry and lab methods
2. Cell structure, transport, and energy
3. DNA, inheritance, and genetic variation
4. Evolution and natural selection
5. Ecology, ecosystems, and human impact
6. Human body systems and homeostasis

Required Materials
Students need a notebook, folder, pencils, colored pencils, and a charged school device. Lab goggles and gloves will be provided when needed.

Late Work Policy
Assignments may be submitted up to one week late for partial credit. Lab safety reflections and major projects require teacher approval if submitted late.
